What Does It Look Like to Be a Marathon Finisher?

Jenna Bellassai
2018-10-07

I was planning to run a half marathon this November. I expected that the most difficult part of
the training would be coaching myself to have the endurance to keep running even when I felt tired.
However, the reality was that the most difficult part was figuring out how to train enough to run
the race without injuring myself.

Unfortunately, I injured myself…Though I won’t be able to run the race, I can still do some
data analysis about runners from the comfort of my couch.

Today, I’m going to look at the dataset of 2017 Boston marathon finishers.
I’m going to be using pandas and Chart.js.

First, let’s look at the ages of the finishers.



Next, let’s look at a breakdown of finishers’ ages by gender:



It’s interesting how the majority of runners 20-30 were female, but the majority of runners 40-50, 50-60, and 60-70 were male.

Our sample size here is maybe not as large as I’d like; I’m under the impression that I’m looking at the complete set of runners who finished the Boston marathon in 2017, but I’m wishing I could see finishers from five or ten years of the marathon to see if these demographic statistics have changed over time, or at least to have a larger number of data points.
